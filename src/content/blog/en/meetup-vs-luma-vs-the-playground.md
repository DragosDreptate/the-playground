Looking for a platform to host events and build your community? Choosing between Meetup, Luma and free alternatives can be confusing. This comparison breaks down the strengths and weaknesses of each platform to help you decide.

## The landscape in 2026

Organising community events has never been easier. But not all platforms are created equal. **Some are event-centric** (you create an event, people show up, then leave), while others are **community-centric** (people join a group and keep coming back).

This distinction is fundamental: it determines whether your attendees will return or whether you'll start from scratch every time.

## Meetup: the community pioneer

Meetup invented the concept of local online groups. Its community model is solid: you create a group, people join it, and they get notified about upcoming events. That's exactly the right model.

The problem? **Meetup charges organisers** (starting at $15/month) and its interface hasn't evolved in years. Registration requires a full account, which hurts conversion.

### Strengths

- Proven community model (group → members → recurring events)
- Large existing user base
- Local discovery (directory by city and topic)

### Weaknesses

- Mandatory organiser subscription ($15-25/month)
- Dated design and UX
- Heavy registration (full account creation before joining an event)
- Limited data export

## Luma: the premium experience

Luma revolutionised the event page. Polished design, one-click registration, seamless calendar integration. It has become the standard for tech and creative events.

But Luma is **event-centric, not community-centric**. There's no concept of a persistent group. Each event is isolated. Your attendees don't "join" anything — they register for a one-off event, and that's it.

> With Luma, you start from zero with every event. No retention, no community growing over time.

### Strengths

- Premium event page (the market benchmark)
- Frictionless registration (email only)
- Native calendar integration
- Modern, clean design

### Weaknesses

- No persistent community (event-centric)
- Commission on paid events
- No "group" or "community" page
- Your attendees are one-time registrants, not members

## The Playground: the best of both worlds

The Playground combines **Meetup's community model** with **Luma's premium experience**, all **100% free**:

- **Persistent community** — your attendees join your community, not just an event
- **Premium event pages** — polished design, frictionless registration, social proof
- **Zero commission** — only Stripe fees on paid events
- **Full export** — your data belongs to you (CSV, members, history)
- **Hosted in Europe** — native GDPR compliance

### The journey that changes everything

On The Playground, when someone registers for your event, they automatically become a member of your community. After the event, they see the next meetups, the other members, your group's identity. They come back naturally.

It's the missing link between Meetup and Luma: **retention** without **friction**.
